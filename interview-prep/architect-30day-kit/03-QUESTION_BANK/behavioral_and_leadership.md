# Behavioral and Leadership Question Bank

Focus on executive clarity, ownership, and influence across stakeholders.

## Leadership and Influence

1. Tell me about a time you influenced a major architecture decision without direct authority.
2. Describe a difficult stakeholder conflict and how you resolved it.
3. Give an example where you changed strategy after new evidence emerged.
4. How do you align engineering and business leaders on architecture trade-offs?
5. Describe how you build high-performing architecture teams.

## Delivery and Execution

1. Tell me about a high-risk program you stabilized.
2. Describe a modernization effort that initially failed. What did you change?
3. How do you make build-vs-buy decisions in enterprise programs?
4. Share a case where you had to deliver under severe time constraints.
5. How do you maintain architecture quality while accelerating delivery?

## People and Mentoring

1. How have you coached senior engineers into architecture roles?
2. Describe a time you gave difficult feedback that improved outcomes.
3. How do you handle persistent underperformance in a critical role?
4. What is your mentoring framework for architecture excellence?
5. How do you create a culture of accountability in distributed teams?

## Failure and Learning

1. Tell me about your biggest architecture mistake and what changed after that.
2. Describe an incident where your assumptions were wrong.
3. How do you conduct postmortems and institutionalize lessons learned?
4. Tell me about a decision that was right technically but wrong organizationally.
5. How do you handle criticism from senior peers or client leadership?

## STAR Template (Use for all answers)

- Situation: define context and stakes
- Task: your responsibility and constraints
- Action: what you did and why
- Result: measurable outcome and impact
- Reflection: what you learned and how you institutionalized it
